Special Rawon

An Indonesian dish originating from East Java.

It is a beef stew with a thick black broth derived from the use of kluwek (kepayang fruit). The rawon soup has a rich and savory taste, with a distinctive aroma from spices such as shallots, garlic, galangal, lemongrass, and lime leaves. The beef used is usually the brisket or ribs, which are cooked until tender. Rawon is usually served with steamed rice, bean sprouts, salted egg and crackers.

Sop Buntut

This is an Indonesian soup dish that uses oxtail as the main ingredient.

Oxtail is cooked until tender in a rich broth, which is usually made from a mixture of spices such as garlic, shallots, nutmeg, cloves and peppercorns. Oxtail soup is often served with additional vegetables such as carrots, potatoes and spring onions.

Tahu Campur

Tahu campur is an Indonesian dish consisting of fried tofu, fresh vegetables, rice cake, and beef in a savory petis sauce.

This dish is usually served with additional crackers and fried onions to give it a crunchy texture. The distinctive petis sauce gives it a unique and appetizing taste.

Iga Penyet

Indonesian dish consisting of beef ribs that are cooked until tender, then battered or beaten until slightly flattened.

The tender ribs are then served with spicy and savory sambal terasi. This dish is usually served with warm white rice, fresh vegetables such as cucumber, tomatoes, and basil leaves, and crackers as a complement. The savory taste of the tender ribs combined with the spicy sambal creates a very delicious and appetizing combination of flavors.

Rujak Cingur

A typical dish from Surabaya, Indonesia, consisting of sliced cingur (cow's nose), fresh vegetables, fruits, and lontong.

All these ingredients are doused with a savory and slightly spicy petis seasoning. The dish is usually served with additional crackers and fried onions to give it a crunchy texture. Rujak cingur has a unique and rich taste, combining sweet, sour, savory and spicy flavors in one dish.
